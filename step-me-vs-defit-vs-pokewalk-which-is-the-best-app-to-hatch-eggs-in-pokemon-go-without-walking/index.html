Step Me vs DeFit vs Pokewalk - which is the best app to hatch eggs in Pokemon Go without walking?

There are apps which count steps from Google Fit and give you rewards: eggs in Pokemon Go, gifts in Toss etc. And there are also couple of apps which adds workouts like walking or running to Google Fit without real training: Step Me, DeFit, Pokewalk etc. So the question is: which app for adding steps to Google Fit is the best? Here are the comparison of three most popular (100,000+ downloads in Google Play) and most reliable (4.0+ rating in Google Play) apps to do that.

Step Me

Rating in Google Play: 4,6
 4.6/5

Step Me uses most advanced algorithm to add real human walks and runs. There is no counter nor work in background, because Step Me adds workouts from past to now. It also allows to add multiple workouts at once, because it uses smart algorithm to place workouts in time in such way that the are not overlapping each other. App is light and easy to use. It is superior over their two competitors.

Pros:

  • no counter which limits the user nor work in background
  • allows to add multiple workouts at once
  • light app and easy to use
  • the newest and fastest growing
  • there are no limits in the premium version, so many steps (even milions) can be added very quickly… 
 

Cons:

  • …which makes that the user should add them responsibly – just don’t be greedy
 

Our verdict: 5/5

DeFit

Rating in Google Play: 4.4
 4.4/5

DeFit was the first app which adds simulated training to Google Fit, so it’s not surprise that its algorithm is oldest and weakest (many users are banned in PoGo after using DeFit). However many users still use it, because they just don’t know better apps like Step Me or Pokewalk. DeFit uses „now to the future” algorithm, which forces user to wait untill next workout will be added. There is counter limiting the user from adding more than one workout in a row. DeFit works in the background, which drains the battery of a device. It is light app, but its UI is not helpful to understand how it works (no manual nor button to add the workout).

Pros:

  • light app
 

Cons:

  • oldest and weakest algorithm (many users reported thet they were banned in PoGo using this app)
  • steps are added very slowly
  • cannot add more than one workout in a row
  • drains battery, because it continuously works in the background
  • UI is not intuitive and there is no explaination how this app works 
 

Our verdict: 3/5

Pokewalk

Rating in Google Play: 4,1
 4.1/5

Pokewalk is newer than DeFit and has a bit better algorithm then its predecessor, however it looses when compared with its successor, Step Me. Pokewalk uses „now to the future” algorithm like DeFit, which makes that no more than one workout can be added in a row (however trainings can be queued). It also works in background. The app is light and contains manual with explaination how it works. Unfortunatelly workouts from Pokewalk are not correctly visible in Google Fit.

Pros:

  • light app and easy to use
  • slightly better algorithm than in DeFit…
 

Cons:

  • …but worse algorithm than in Step Me
  • steps are added very slowly
  • cannot add more than one workout in a row
  • workouts from Pokewalk are not correctly visible in Google Fit
 

Our verdict: 4/5

 

Form this comparison it’s clear that Step Me is the easiest and the most efficient app for adding walks and runs to Google Fit. Therefore Step Me is also the best app to hatch eggs in Pokemon Go without walking.
